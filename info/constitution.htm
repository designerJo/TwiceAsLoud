Draft constitution
March 1998






Below is the first draft of the Twice as Loud constitution, which is currently under discussion.
Objectives
To produce consistently high quality work for our audiences; and to provide an opportunity for each member to stretch him/herself and continue to learn.
  
1		   
Membership
1.1 Membership of the company is confirmed by payment of a subscription to the company of £50 per quarter (£25 concessions). Subscriptions can be varied, however, if the company agrees.
See the people pages for details of current members
1.2 Membership confers on each person:
  • The right to vote at meetings
  • The right to initiate projects
  • The right to act as a programme director by rotation
  • The right to be auditioned for parts
1.3 Guest actors/directors etc. can be brought in for specific projects but do not pay subscriptions and do not have the right to initiate work.
1.4 New members can be brought in or existing members asked to leave by vote of agreement of three quarters of the company.
  
2		   
Meetings
2.1 Volunteers will be asked for at each meeting to act as Chairman and secretary for that meeting
2.2 Anyone can call a meeting and the meeting will be quorate provided that at least 50% of the company (currently 7) is present.
2.3 Decisions should be made by the majority vote of those present and including any proxy votes from those not able to be there. Certain decisions require the consent by vote of three quarters of the company, specifically:
  • Any change to the constitution
  • Any membership issues
2.4 We should have regular workshops so that we can work together as a company plus monthly meetings and social events.
  
3		   
Programmes
3.1 based on the financial resources available, the whole company should decide at the beginning of each year how many productions it can afford to finance. If members have additional projects which they wish to stage outside the company, then they can do so but will have to finance them independently and the programme would not be publicised as a Twice as Loud production.
3.2 Each programme should have at least two programme directors whose responsibility will be to decide the theme of the programme and act as final decision maker on the make-up of the programme and programme order. The producers will also be responsible for organising the programme. If the producers cannot agree with each other, then decisions will revert to the company.
3.3 Producers will be rotated to allow every member of the company, if they wish, to undertake a challenging role, either as actor, director or writer.
3.4 Members will be able to submit projects to the programme directors which fit in with the theme. Once a programme has been put together, it should be formerly approved by three quarter vote of the company. No commitment to booking theatres should, however, be made without the approval of the company, though non-binding bookings can be made to secure slots.
3.5 Casting should be the responsibility of the director or originator (who may be an actor or writer) of each project. Decisions should be made on the basis of choosing the best person for the part and if a number of people are interested in a part, either from within the company or from outside, then the decision should be made by audition. Twice as Loud members have the right to ask to be auditioned for parts. Guest actors to be invited to perform should be notified to the group as a matter of courtesy.
  
4		   
Treasury matters
4.1 Subscriptions should be paid to the Treasurers for each quarter by the following dates:

1 Jan to 31 Mar by 1 March
1 Apr to 30 Jun by 1 June
1 Jul to 30 Sep by 1 September
1 Oct to 31 Dec by 1 December

Where possible members are urged to take out standing orders
4.2 Only the treasurers can issue cheques on behalf of the company.
4.3 Expenses can be reclaimed for each production up to a limit set by the treasurers for each production. Anything over that amount has to be approved at a meeting.
4.4 Rehearsal space in general should not be reimbursed unless there are special circumstances given the size of cast etc. and availability of rooms and this has been agreed in advance. Preview and dress rehearsal space will be paid by the company.
