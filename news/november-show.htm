Wanda's Visit by Christopher Durang. 
An old school friend comes to visit Jim and Marsha and creates havoc in their lives when she turns into the guest from hell. Originally produced as part of the six-play evening, Durang/Durang, Wanda’s Visit deftly uses Durang’s hallmark eccentric comedy to tell an unsettling story of unfulfilled relationships.

Like when you spin the first showing of this new play by Caroline Ross.
It is the arrival of Beatrice’s new lodger, Mary, who sparks off a sea-change in the relationship between Beatrice and her sister. With a combination of black humour and stark poetry, Caroline Ross brings the action to its inevitable and yet unexpected conclusion. The play owes its origins to characters and improvised scenes devised under the direction of Craig Snelling at Morley College.
